AETHELGARD

A stitched-together corpse of a ruined era — and everything that survived it.

Long before mortal history, the world was a borderless realm of godlike entities waging eternal war. Their final conflict—The Great Shattering—tore reality apart. When the dust settled, only five goddesses remained. They forged a bitter, silent truce, grafted the shattered fragments of the dead world into one continent, then buried themselves in eternal slumber. Aethelgard was born not out of love—but desperation.

The Sweet Myth The world was born from the perfect harmony of the Five Goddesses—a beautiful gift created out of love for their descendants.

The Dark Truth Aethelgard is a stitched-together, radioactive corpse of a ruined era. The goddesses did not create paradise. They built a lifeboat.

II

The Five Goddesses

Before the Great Shattering, dozens of divine entities walked the Old World. Only five survived. Exhausted beyond measure, they grafted the broken fragments of the dead world together—each weaving one inviolable Law into the new reality—then buried themselves in eternal slumber. They do not answer prayers. They simply sleep.

Aethelgard — Law of Spatial Stability
Domain: Earth's Core · Gravity · Physical Integrity

"The world is finite. It cannot grow or expand. The land shall hold together by her eternal will."

She forcibly grafted the shattered continental fragments together. Every mountain range is a scar from her stitching. Every Seam Zone is a wound she could not fully close. The continent was named for her.

Valerion — Law of Linear Time
Domain: Time · Irreversibility · The Permanence of Regret

"The past cannot be repeated. It cannot be changed. Regrets are permanent. Time flows only forward."

The cruelest mercy. In the Old World, gods could rewind mistakes and resurrect the fallen—and this bred catastrophe. Valerion ensured no resurrection exists in Aethelgard. The dead stay dead.

Nyxara — Law of Life & The Immune System
Domain: Flora · Fauna · The World's Biological Defenses

"Life shall flourish and defend itself. All living things are part of a sacred, self-correcting balance."

Mother of monsters. She created the vast ecology of Aethelgard—not out of malice, but necessity. Behemoths are her titans. Surface monsters are her immune cells. She co-created the Leviathans as the final wall against the Void.

Thanatos — Law of Absolute Finality
Domain: Death · Souls · The Eternal End

"There is no reincarnation. No resurrection. No afterlife. Dead souls disintegrate into Aether. Everything must end."

The most misunderstood of the Five. In the Old World, immortality bred monstrous apathy. Thanatos ensured that all beings—mortal or not—would eventually face an absolute end.

Mystra-Thoth — Law of Magic & Logic
Domain: Aether · The Weave · Knowledge · Magic as Science

"Magic is not luck. It is not faith. It is an exact, predictable science. An identical pull from the Weave always produces an identical result."

The great systematizer. She wove the Aether Weave—the world-spanning network governing all magic. Aether is recycled soul energy. Every spell cast consumes the remnants of a dead being.

III

The Weave & Aether

The Aether Weave is the invisible skeleton of Aethelgard—a world-spanning network of magical threads designed by Mystra-Thoth. Magic here is not faith. It is an exact, predictable science. Every spell follows the same principle: reach into the Weave, pull a specific thread, shape it with will, release it. Identical input, identical output.

Internal vs. External Weave

Internal Aether Weave — The safe, personal reservoir within the caster's body. The primary power source for almost all mortals. One's Internal Weave capacity determines their Circle.

External Aether Weave — Touching the Greater Weave of the world itself. Grants immense power but attracts Old World grudges, violently accelerates aging, and causes catastrophic side effects. Only ancient High Races can wield it safely.

VII

Dungeons — The Wounds of Aethelgard

Dungeons are the physical mouths of reality scars—festering wounds where the dead Old World bleeds into the stitched New. The Laws of the Five Goddesses weaken or reverse inside. To descend is to travel backward through the history of a dead era.

The Dungeon does not want explorers to die of starvation. Every layer possesses a unique, self-sustaining food source—a twisted chance to survive. Scholars debate whether this is the Dungeon desperately wanting mortals to witness the forgotten tragedy of the Old World… or simply wanting to prolong their psychological suffering.

— Guildhall Research Division, Year 2390 EK

Layers 300–500 km: The False Heaven

A 200 km corridor of perfect, idyllic paradise. Zero monsters. Friendly "Echo" inhabitants. The Dungeon's ultimate trap: the Old World's revenge through embrace, not killing. The Fusion Mechanism: Observation → Adaptation → Acceptance → Forgetting → Fusion. Victims become Echoes forever.

Layers 500–10,000+ km: The War of the Gods

A perfect, infinitely looping replica of the Primordial War. Non-linear time. Mountain-sized god corpses. At 8,000–10,000 km: the Five Goddesses themselves are visible, desperately attempting to stitch reality back together—forever trapped in the moment of their greatest failure.

Surface Monsters — Nyxara's children. Impossibly diverse; no fixed bestiary exists. Some species can be tamed. They carry the Immune Instinct: they will abandon any fight to destroy dungeon spawn on sight.

Dungeon Monsters — Born from Old World grudges, not biology. Shallow layers breed mindless chaos; deep layers spawn sentient, reproductive ancient nightmares.
